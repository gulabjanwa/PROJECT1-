A.P.J. Abdul-Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

The Indian National Congress (INC), colloquially the Congress Party or simply the Congress, is a political party in India with deep roots in most regions of India. Founded on 28 December 1885, it was the first modern nationalist movement to emerge in the British Empire in Asia and Africa.[a][29] From the late 19th century, and especially after 1920, under the leadership of Mahatma Gandhi, the Congress became the principal leader of the Indian independence movement.[30] The Congress led India to independence from the United Kingdom,[b][31][c][32] and significantly influenced other anti-colonial nationalist movements in the British Empire.[d][29]

Biographies

  • Kalam was the youngest of four brothers and one sister in his family.
  • His ancestors had been wealthy Marakayar traders and landowners, with numerous properties and large tracts of land.
  • Kalam was noted for his integrity and his simple lifestyle.
  • He was extremely close to his elder siblings and their extended families throughout his life,
  • He never owned a television, and was in the habit of rising at 6:30 or 7 a.m. and sleeping by 2 a.m
